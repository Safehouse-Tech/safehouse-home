How do we help Social Services ?

Providing care within the community to vulnerable people and supporting the NHS.

Safehouse is ideal for Social Services who wish to support for carers, monitoring service users who require additional help. Our social care package has been designed to reduce the anxiety of employers and employees by creating an open platform in which to communicate and oversea care plans and thus taking pressure off the NHS.

By using Viper (SaaS), all those involved in an individual’s care will be able to view the environmental conditions within residents properties such as the temperature, humidity, CO2 levels, light levels, noise levels and general air quality of the property. As well as the environmental readings. Carers and family alike will also be able to track scheduled care visits and monitor service users daily activity, thus have a greater understanding and awareness of their health and well-being.

With Safehouse you can install a variety of levels of care from basic low cost monitoring sensors to personal alarms and all of which are supported by our mobile app and online portal which can be accessed by a number of individuals responsible for supporting service users.

All the equipment arrives preconfigured in a single ‘plug and play’ package, where all the sensors and devices are geospatially tagged, allocated to a room or service user, and labelled ready for installation.

Installation does not require a physical engineer; the devices simply plug into main power sockets, or indeed use strong adhesive pads.

Care home managers and staff alike are be able to remotely monitor the internal conditions of residents rooms and common areas, using the Viper (SaaS) dashboard.

The Safehouse app and also give carers the ability to receive instant notifications from triggered devices, and locate an individual's whereabouts at all times of the day and night.

Panic buttons can be personally worn with a lanyard or clipped onto clothing and run off Bluetooth Low Energy. In addition Panic Buttons can be attached to surfaces around the home in the event of a fall or if the user is not wearing their personal alarm.

Service users' family members can also access the same information as the Social Services through Viper (SaaS) and the Safehouse App, creating a clear path of communication between those responsible for issuing care.
